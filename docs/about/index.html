About Me

Welcome to my corner of the digital world! I'm Xiaoyou Wu, a Computer Engineering student at Georgia Tech with a deep passion for bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical innovation. My journey in engineering is driven by curiosity about how things work and a desire to create solutions that make a meaningful impact.

My fascination with technology began early, but it was during my studies at Georgia Tech that I discovered my love for the intersection of hardware and software. From designing PCBs for flight computers to exploring the delicate world of soft robotics, I'm constantly amazed by the possibilities that emerge when creativity meets engineering rigor.

What Drives Me

I believe that the most exciting innovations happen at the boundaries between disciplines. Whether it's developing bio-inspired robotic systems or creating avionics solutions for aerospace applications, I'm drawn to projects that require both technical depth and creative problem-solving.

My work in soft robotics particularly excites me because it represents a paradigm shift from rigid, precise machines to adaptive, compliant systems that can work safely alongside humans and navigate unpredictable environments.

Beyond Engineering

When I'm not immersed in circuits and code, you might find me exploring new technologies, reading about the latest developments in robotics and AI, or working on personal projects that challenge me to learn new skills. I'm also passionate about sharing knowledge and helping others discover the joy of engineering.

This website serves as both a portfolio of my work and a platform for sharing insights from my engineering journey. I hope you'll find something here that inspires or helps you in your own pursuits.

Looking Forward

As I continue my studies and prepare for the next phase of my career, I'm excited about the opportunities to contribute to cutting-edge research and development. Whether in academia or industry, I'm committed to pushing the boundaries of what's possible in engineering and technology.

Thank you for taking the time to learn about my story. I'd love to connect with fellow engineers, researchers, and anyone passionate about technology and innovation.
